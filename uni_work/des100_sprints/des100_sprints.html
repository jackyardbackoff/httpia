In my DES100 course, the lecturers forced us to design lamps in 9-day sprints. The assignments featured here were called 'cut light' and 'printed light': we used 3d printing and laser cutting to craft our pieces.

My first assignment was 'cut light'. I wanted to make an organic-looking lamp: the medium really restricted this, though. Lasercutting is not natural or lawful. I decided to create organic patterns through random circle gaps in my lamp, and create a rounded base using a holey design.

Lazercut was cool to work with! Below are some pictures and a video from the sprint:

initial brianstorm
final design, white
final design, black

In 'printed light', I decided to make a diatom-inspired organic lamp. I liked the sciencey, bacterial (??? hehehheh) shapes of the particles. I made the forms by using the sculpt & hole tools on my 3D software. These techniques were very simple: I was too dumb to do anything more difficult, lol :-(

I loved how these prints turned out: they're very simple, but cool AF to me!
