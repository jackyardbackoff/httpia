These are two illustrations I produced for DESIGN FOR CONSERVATION. We were tasked with pitching a concept to conserve biodiversity within the CBD. I drew these as part of our group's eco-avian diversity project.

These were from two final assignments. The earlier concept (right) is of a waterfront with greater green corridors, a restored wetland, living walls/roofs and more natural recreational spaces.

I really enjoyed the research for this project! Learning about the Domain's wetland, Aotearoa NZ's existing conservation projects, and Berlin's urban sheep were highlights. I also enjoyed thinking about how people would be affected by our design: e.g. how we'd get people interested, or create social change. I'd love to work more with this kind of thing. It was very engaging & I loved talking with my lecturers, tutors & classmates :-D.
