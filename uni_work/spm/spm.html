This was for an assignment titled "CRAFTING WITH WASTE". We were tasked with researching for, developing, and promoting a musical instrument made from trash. I remember walking around the CBD, inspecting and collecting pieces of trash, feeling really sneaky and gross. I got confronted like twice for littering (which I wasn't doing!).

I wasn't really feeling the hands-on vibe of it all, and wanted to do stuff with arduino; I made a trash enclosure for my instrument to fulfil the brief, and then fucked off to the FABLAB electronics corner. I had many ideas of how to make my synth, but settled on using light input to manipulate the tone by flailing around (like a theremin!).

This assignment was doomed from the start. I would've liked to be more ambitious - instead, I relied heavily on the FABLAB dudes & took a bunch of shortcuts. The end result isn't really how I pictured. Also my lecturer kept leading me down whimsical rabbitholes aughhhghh!! I still enjoyed this assignment though, and would love to explore synth stuff later on. Experimenting with programming languages to manipulate sound, or looking at re-working this instrument, would both be super cool :-P
